Charanka Solar Park (Patan district, Gujarat)

It was inaugurated in 2012 by honorable prime minister Narendra Modi.It became the world's third largest photo voltaic power station.
Installed generation capacity is at about 615 MW at present, having been commissioned by 31 developers in the Solar Park.
So far, the park has witnessed investments of Rs 5,365 crore and generated 3,441 million units till date.

ONGC Wind Power Plant (Kutch, Gujarat)

ONGC has created a dedicated Renewable Energy Cell (REC).
51 MW Wind power plant consisting of 34 numbers of 1.5 MW Wind turbine generators is in operation in Kutch Gujarat Since 2008.
102 MW wind power plant comprising of 49 numbers of 2.1 MW Wind Turbine Generators was commissioned in September 2015 in Jaisalmer District Rajasthan at a cost of Rs. 562 Crore.

Sardar Sarovar Dam (Hydel Energy)

Sardar Sarovar Dam (SSD), on the Indian Narmada river, is located in the village of Kevadia in the state of Gujarat.
The Sardar Sarovar project was a vision of the first deputy prime minister of India, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.On occasion of his 67th birthday, Prime Minister Narendra Modi has inaugurated the Sardar Sarovar Dam on the Narmada river on 17 September 2017.
The dam's main power plant houses six 200 MW Francis pump-turbines to generate electricity and include a pumped-storage capabilityThe total installed capacity of the power facilities is 1,450 MW.

Sankheda Biomass Power Plant (Vadodra, Gujarat)

It is 1.2 MW biomass gasification power generation project currently operation al in a small scale.
It is responsible for 6,256 metric tonnes CO2 equivalent per annum. As biomass is carbon neutral no additional Greenhouse gas is emitted into the atmosphere due to this project activity.
Since the project is located in a village assists in alleviation of poverty to certain extent by generating both direct and indirect employment in the area of skilled/unskilled jobs for regular operation and maintenance of the power plant.

Kakrapar Atomic Power Station (Moti Cher, Gujarat)

Kakrapar Atomic Power Station is a nuclear power station in India, which lies in the proximity of Surat and Tapi river in the state of Gujarat.
KAPS currently operates two PHWRs, the first of which (Unit-1) began commercial operations on 6 May 1993. Unit-2 followed two years later in September 1995.
As of 28 February 2003, KARP's cumulative energy generation since its commercial start date was 24,892 million units (MUs)
